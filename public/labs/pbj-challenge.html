The Peanut Butter & Jelly Challenge

Write the instructions. The machine follows them exactly — no guessing, no filling in the blanks.

Why you're doing this

This is how a Business Analyst earns. A BA takes something people "just know how to do" and writes it down so precisely that a computer — or a developer who's never seen it — can do it the same way every time.

In ServiceNow, that's the whole job: turn a messy human process into clean, ordered steps the platform can run. You already do this. Every SOP you've followed or written is requirements work. This just makes the skill visible.

The task

Write the steps to make a peanut butter and jelly sandwich.

The catch: a machine follows your steps literally, top to bottom. It has never made a sandwich. It assumes nothing. If a step is missing, out of order, or unclear — the sandwich fails.

Don't rush to "finish." Add as many steps as you need. Drag them until the order is true. There's no set number — figuring that out is the work.

    If one of your steps says "spread the peanut butter," the machine stops and asks: with what? how much? on which side? Open the jar first — was that a step? The gaps you just found are the exact gaps a Business Analyst hunts for in real requirements, before a single thing gets built.

    Strong requirements share three traits:

    CompleteOrderedUnambiguous

    Complete means nothing is assumed. Ordered means the sequence is true — step 4 can't depend on something you didn't do until step 7. Unambiguous means one meaning only, no "they'll figure it out."

    That's the muscle behind every ServiceNow build: the platform does exactly what the steps say — not what you meant. Learn to write the sandwich and you can write the workflow.
