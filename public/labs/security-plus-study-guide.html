Your CompTIA Security+ Study Guide

The exact path to Security+ (SY0-701) — mapped to your Rich Off Tech course + practice quiz, domain by domain.

What Security+ proves: that you can secure systems, spot and respond to threats, and speak the language of every security team. It's the baseline cert for cleared and federal cyber roles (DoD 8570/8140 approved) — the one that gets your resume past the filter. Exam: SY0-701, up to 90 questions (multiple-choice + performance-based), 90 minutes, 750/900 to pass, taken from home with remote proctoring. Your program includes the exam voucher plus a retake voucher.

Step 1 — Set up your practice

Security+ is concept-heavy with hands-on performance questions. Three things to have ready before you start:

  1. Your ROT practice quiz — your main feedback loop. You're ready for a domain when you're scoring 85%+ on it.
  2. Acronym + port flashcards — Security+ lives on acronyms (CIA, AAA, GRC, SIEM, IDS/IPS) and common ports (22, 53, 80/443, 389/636, 3389). Drill them daily from day one.
  3. A little hands-on — a free TryHackMe or a home VM (Kali + a Windows target) makes the performance-based questions click. Optional but it sticks faster.

Step 2 — The 5 domains (and where to drill each)

SY0-701 is 5 domains. Study the domain, then drill that exact domain on your Rich Off Tech quiz until you're at 85%+ before moving on. The % is how heavily each is weighted on the exam.

1

General Security Concepts Quiz: General Security Concepts~12% of exam

The CIA triad, AAA, security controls (technical/managerial/operational/physical and preventive/detective/corrective…), zero trust, the basics of cryptography (symmetric vs asymmetric, hashing, PKI, certificates), and change management. The vocabulary the rest of the exam is built on.

2

Threats, Vulnerabilities & Mitigations Quiz: Threats, Vulnerabilities & Mitigations~22%

Threat actors and motivations, attack surfaces and vectors, malware types, social engineering, application/network/cloud vulnerabilities, indicators of an attack, and the mitigations that shut each down. The heaviest "know your enemy" domain.

3

Security Architecture Quiz: Security Architecture~18%

Securing the architecture: cloud vs on-prem, network design (segmentation, DMZ, VPN, firewalls, zero-trust), secure protocols, data protection (encryption at rest/in transit, classification, DLP), and resilience/recovery (backups, redundancy, high availability).

4

Security Operations Quiz: Security Operations~28% — the biggest

The day-to-day: hardening, identity & access management (MFA, SSO, least privilege), monitoring (SIEM, logging, alerting), vulnerability management, incident response, digital forensics, and automation. This is the largest domain — give it the most time.

5

Security Program Management & Oversight Quiz: Security Program Management~20%

Governance, risk management (risk assessment, treatment, registers), third-party/vendor risk, compliance and audits, security policies and standards, and security awareness. The "running the program" / GRC domain — heavy on definitions.

Step 3 — Pick your pace: 30, 60, or 90 days

Rich Off Tech is a break-into-tech-fast program — no plan runs longer than 90 days. You and your coach pick the lane that fits your schedule on your first call. Same 5 domains, same 85%+ target per quiz domain.

30

30-Day Sprint ~2–3 hrs/day

All-in. ~1 domain every 4–5 days + daily flashcards, then a week of full practice exams. Exam at the end of the month.

60

60-Day Standard ~1–1.5 hrs/day

The balanced lane. ~1 domain every 8–10 days with time to re-drill weak spots and run 2–3 timed practice exams before you sit.

90

90-Day Around-Life ~3–4 hrs/week

Built around full-time work and family. ~1 domain every 2 weeks, steady, no burnout. Still certified inside one quarter.

Whatever lane you pick: study the domain → drill that quiz domain to 85%+ → next domain. Hit 85% across all 5 and you're exam-ready. Your coach adjusts your plan anytime your schedule changes — it never stretches past 90 days.
