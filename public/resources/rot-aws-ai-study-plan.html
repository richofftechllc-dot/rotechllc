📚 The 5 exam domains (AIF-C01)

DomainWeightWhat it covers
1. Fundamentals of AI & ML20%AI vs ML vs deep learning, training vs inference, supervised/unsupervised, model evaluation
2. Fundamentals of Generative AI24%Foundation models, LLMs, tokens, prompts, embeddings, Amazon Bedrock basics
3. Applications of Foundation Models28%Prompt engineering, RAG, fine-tuning, agents, Bedrock/SageMaker JumpStart, Amazon Q
4. Guidelines for Responsible AI14%Bias, fairness, transparency, hallucinations, human-in-the-loop, guardrails
5. Security, Compliance & Governance14%IAM for AI, data privacy, model/data governance, AWS shared-responsibility for AI

🗓️ Your 30 / 60 / 90-day plan

PhaseFocus
First third
Foundation		Domains 1 & 2 — get AI/ML vocabulary cold (supervised vs unsupervised, training vs inference, over/underfitting), then GenAI core: foundation models, LLMs, tokens, prompts, embeddings. Open the Amazon Bedrock console and actually send a prompt to a model. Goal: you can explain what a foundation model is and call one.
Middle third
Applications		Domain 3 (the heaviest, 28%) — prompt engineering, RAG, fine-tuning vs prompt-tuning, agents, Bedrock vs SageMaker JumpStart, Amazon Q Developer/Business, and the pre-built services (Comprehend, Rekognition, Textract, Transcribe). Know which service for which job — the exam lives here. Goal: you can pick the right AWS AI service for a use case.
Final third
Responsible AI + Pass		Domains 4 & 5 — bias/fairness, hallucinations, human-in-the-loop, guardrails, plus IAM/data privacy/governance for AI. Then drill 200+ practice questions in the ROT quiz engine to a steady 80%+, schedule the exam in this window, and take it. Goal: booked and passed.
