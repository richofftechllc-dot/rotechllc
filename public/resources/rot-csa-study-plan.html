ServiceNow CSA — Your Study Plan 🤎

The Certified System Administrator path, start to exam. Follow it in order. Your AI tutors (Bo & Flo) are in Discord the whole way.

🎯 What the CSA proves

You can run the ServiceNow platform: users, roles & groups, forms & UI policies, tables & data, workflows, imports, and the update sets that ship changes. It's your front door into the six-figure ServiceNow lane (admin → business analyst → developer).

🖥️ First move — Set up your PDI (Personal Developer Instance)

Your PDI is your own free ServiceNow instance to practice in. Get it before you study anything else.

1
Go to developer.servicenow.com and create a free account.
2
Top-right → Request Instance → pick the latest release. Wait ~5 min for it to spin up.
3
Save your instance URL, admin username, and password somewhere safe (you'll get a temp password — change it).
4
Log in. In the top filter navigator, type System Definition > Tables — if you see the table list, you're in. You're ready to build.
5
⚠️ PDIs go to sleep after ~10 days of inactivity — log in weekly so it doesn't get reclaimed.

🗓️ Your 30 / 60 / 90-day plan

Three phases: learn the platform, build in it, then drill & pass. Flex the days around your life — the order is what matters.

PhaseFocus
Days 1–30
Foundation		Set up your PDI + platform tour. Master User Administration (users, groups, roles, ACLs, impersonation) and Data & Tables (tables, fields, dictionary, references). Start CSA practice questions in the quiz engine. Goal: you can navigate the platform and explain the data model.
Days 31–60
Build		Forms & UI policies, Import Sets + Transform Maps (coalesce + load data), Update Sets (capture + move a change), Flow Designer + notifications, and the Service Catalog. Build each one in your PDI, don't just read it. Goal: you've actually shipped a change end-to-end.
Days 61–90
Drill & Pass		Reporting, homepages & dashboards, then review every weak spot. Run 200+ practice questions in the ROT quiz engine until you're steady at 75%+. Schedule the exam for the back half of this window and take it. Goal: booked and passed.

Moving faster? Compress it — this same order works as a 6-week sprint if you can put in the hours. Slower is fine too; just don't skip the build phase.
